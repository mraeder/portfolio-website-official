Money Trees



Summary


In my coding bootcamp, we had the opportunity to take a project concept and develop it using each of the technologies we learned in the bootcamp. I built Money Trees as a fictitious crowdfunding website for entrepreneurs and creators where users can learn more about Money Trees as a crowdfunding host, explore active campaigns plus their goals and progress, and inquire about starting their own campaign. This project helped me take my new React skills for a test drive!


Video Tour




Challenges


This whole project challenged me to apply everything I learned about React to my own application. I struggled at first with the campaign progress concept, which shows the total money raised. Separating campaigns.js and progress.js made it cleaner and easier to display the progress when clicked, rather than combining the two files. Other considerations that are becoming more habitual for me are making sure to stick with npm or Yarn throughout the whole project, updating/naming exports correctly, and adding the right source paths between files; all small details that could trip up a brand-new developer.
